Why marine engine oils matter

Marine oils must lubricate, cool, control deposits, neutralize acids, and protect against wear — under long duty cycles, variable fuel quality, and strict emissions limits. The right oil reduces unplanned downtime and extends component life across main and auxiliary engines.

Orsa Max 50

Mineral-based marine oil designed to keep engines clean, neutralize acidic byproducts, and protect against wear in demanding service.

Why it works

  • High oxidation & thermal stability for slower oil aging
  • Detergent/dispersant system for deposit & soot control
  • High TBN to neutralize acids and protect against corrosion
  • Anti-wear additives to protect bearings under heavy loads

Typical use cases

  • Low-speed crosshead main engines (per OEM recommendation)
  • Auxiliary equipment where the OEM specifies this grade
  • Operators targeting extended service intervals with analysis

Confirm viscosity grade, BN, and OEM approval before switching formulations. Plan a controlled changeover.

Rudder Max 40

Alkaline, dual-purpose API CF-level oil for medium-speed trunk piston engines operating on heavy residual fuels.

Why it works

  • High oxidation & thermal stability for longer drains
  • Strong detergency/dispersancy to keep systems clean
  • High TBN for acid neutralization and corrosion protection
  • Anti-wear package for bearings and loaded components

Application notes

  • Match BN to fuel sulfur and duty cycle
  • Verify OEM approvals and service letters before use
  • Pair with routine oil analysis to optimize intervals

Suitable for certain low-speed crosshead applications if OEM recommends. Always cross-check specification.

Best practices for marine lubrication

Condition monitoring

  • Routine lab analysis: TBN/TAN, viscosity @40/100 °C, insolubles
  • Watch oxidation/nitration trends and contamination (fuel/water)
  • Adjust drain intervals based on data, not just hours

Handling & changeover

  • Store drums dry, covered; dedicate clean transfer equipment
  • Prevent water/fuel ingress; verify separator/filters
  • Use a controlled procedure for brand/grade changeover
